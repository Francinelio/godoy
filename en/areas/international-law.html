International trade

  • Advice on WTO agreements and the use of their dispute settlement mechanisms and Mercosur's trade and structural regulation.
  • Of the European Union, protagonist in international trade as one of the most active blocks in relation to world trade. The EU, as a member of the World Trade Organization, advocates the progressive liberalization of trade and the elimination of both technical and customs barriers. Nonetheless, while other blocs are making greater progress in this field, the EU continues to use trade defense instruments, including the importance and enforcement of anti-dumping duties against goods imported from outside the EU at prices abnormally low. The number of anti-dumping investigation proceedings initiated by the European Commission has increased significantly over the last two years, from four investigations in 2003 to twenty-five in 2006. The European Commission has recently launched a public reflection and consultation on the application of the instruments. trade defense in the context of a changing global economy.
