Startup & Open Innovation Law

How can we help business?

Business legal feasibility:

  • risk analysis and assessment in disruptive business.

Corporate and financial:

  • drafting of the bylaws;
  • partner agreements;
  • startup participation conditions in incubators and accelerators;
  • recurrent contractual advice;
  • financing rounds;
  • alternative financing;
  • support in due diligence processes.

Asset protection:

  • mapping intangible protection strategy (trademarks, patents, software, databases, know-how) worldwide;
  • record protection;
  • confidentiality agreements;
  • technology development and transfer contracts;
  • licenses and assignment of intangibles;
  • reports on IP rights of startups in acquisition processes;
  • open software;
  • dispute resolution.

Contents:

  • negotiation with management entities
  • advice on advertising campaigns;
  • contracts with influencers;
  • social networks;
  • new forms of advertising;
  • adwords; and
  • Online reputation.

Privacy:

  • advice on information management as an asset, in particular with regard to personal data in business activities;
  • advice on compliance with the new General Data Protection Regulation; and
  • defense in proceedings before regulatory bodies in the various jurisdictions.

Cyber security:

  • legal advice on cybersecurity risk prevention;
  • emergency care in case of cyber attacks;
  • support in the phases following a cyber attack (notifications, communications, complaints and lawsuits).

National and international taxation:

  • revision of the Corporate Income Tax to verify the correct application of the deductions related to international double taxation and technological tax incentives;
  • international tax advice and relocation of people to other countries;
  • personal tax advice of the partner in case of entry of other investors;
  • advice on tax incentives for investment in startups; and
  • support in the correct application of the Non-Resident Income Tax.

Laboral: advisory services in

  • the creation and start-up of new businesses;
  • employment bonuses and aids applicable to start-ups and during their growth phase;
  • the impact of digital transformation on labor relations in teleworking, distance working, smart working phenomena;
  • the implementation of measures for the effective use of technological and communication means in industrial relations;
  • protocols for use of computer tools;
  • video surveillance systems or geolocation devices;
  • incentive plans.

Intellectual property:

  • The most valuable companies in the digital economy build their competitive advantage based on innovation. In this context, sound management of intellectual and industrial property is crucial, as it is the tool that allows us to obtain exclusive rights to the assets in which innovation resides. Among others, we should ask the following questions: How to protect the algorithms? What rights do I need to get for setting up a content platform? Who owns the works created by AI? How to protect big data? What precautions should we take when buying a technology company?
